To Read Our Lore?

Japan had been teetering on the edge for a while. After Karoshi Pharmaceuticals was established in 2078, no one knew just how close to the edge we were. In 2084 the Red Mist first appeared in the far north. By 2097 it had covered the majority of the Island. Karoshi started building air purifiers for the larger cities, but it wasn’t enough. As of today, 2261, Tokyo is the only city left, and only a fraction of its former size. Only two wards remain in the Tokyo Metropolis. The Senzaki Ward holds the majority of Tokyo’s Commons, the Izori ward houses Karoshi’s main factory, along with its executives and military police.

The rest were overrun by ghouls, covered in the mist, or a gruesome combination of the two. It’s not all awful though, in the 2086 Ghoul Response Operations Update, Karoshi announced their first line of commercial assault gear and combat cybernetics, available to anyone for the right price, to protect themselves from the Ghoul threat. This led to the creation of the Hunters, a special class of civilians, taking up a vigilante role to protect the citizens of Tokyo. Karoshi may have their private military and act as the de-facto rulers of the city, but they clearly don’t have any regard for the citizens they “protect”.

Along with the Hunters and Commons, there is one other class in Tokyo. The Tokyo Ten are ten Hunters with exceptional skill, and the most well-known names in all of Tokyo. Their leader, Fumiko, was the first Hunter to be considered more than a protector, but a genuine hero and force for good. She soon set about finding more like-minded individuals to help with her cause. The Tokyo Ten are the closest thing we have to real Superheroes, and they experience the same level of admiration. Taking on entire packs of Ghouls single-handedly, running operations to clean up outside the walls of Tokyo, and secretly operating against Karoshi Pharmaceuticals to take back Japan. The Tokyo Ten have made their base of operations at the northern edge of the Senzaki Ward, which provides an excellent location for expeditions into the ruins of Old Tokyo for supplies.

It has been almost two centuries since the Red Mist first rocked the island to its core, and it’s beginning to come to a head. Ghoul incursions are becoming more and more frequent and Karoshi is slowly tightening its grip on the city, leaving the Commons nowhere to turn. In response, the Ten are ramping up their offensives all over the island. From Karoshi’s main factory in the Izori Ward to the west of the residential areas, they have been monitoring the Ten’s activity. The Ghouls are being pushed back slowly, but there seems to be no end to them, and it’s only a matter of time until the purifiers can’t control the fog that seems to get thicker by the day, and people are starting to question if Karoshi has any intention of actually stopping it. There is one thing that gives the people of the city hope: the promise of hidden treasure. As any good scavenger knows, there are pockets of hidden Karoshi tech all over the islands. Rumors vary about what’s in these caches. Some say it’s experimental tech from before the collapse; armor, weapons, and unique implants that Karoshi was going to sell to the top bidder. Others say it’s old stockpiles of medicine and rations, which by today’s standards would be more valuable than any old-world tech or fancy machines.
